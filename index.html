Information

Frequently Asked
Questions

Transitioning to an adult family home is a significant decision. We aim to provide absolute transparency regarding our care, facilities, and procedures to help you make an informed choice.

What levels of care do you provide?
We offer comprehensive services ranging from basic daily living assistance (bathing, dressing, meal preparation) to specialized memory care and complex medication management, all tailored to the evolving needs of each resident.
What is the staff-to-resident ratio?
To maintain our high standards of personalized attention, we keep a very low staff-to-resident ratio. This ensures our caregivers have the time to provide unhurried support and foster meaningful relationships.
Can you accommodate specific dietary restrictions?
Absolutely. All our meals are home-cooked, and we strictly adhere to prescribed dietary plans including diabetic, low-sodium, pureed, and allergy-specific diets without compromising on taste or nutrition.
Are family members allowed to visit at any time?
We believe family connection is vital. While we have recommended visiting hours to respect the rest periods of all residents, we operate an open-door policy for designated family members to visit their loved ones.
How are medical emergencies handled?
Our staff is thoroughly trained in emergency response protocols, CPR, and First Aid. We coordinate closely with local emergency services and primary physicians, ensuring immediate action and family notification in any urgent situation.
